Step 1: Unit & Learning Focus

Set up your unit basics and map out what students need to learn.

Step 2: Our Team Goal & Reality Check

State your unit goal and talk through potential roadblocks before you begin teaching.

Example: "By Oct 15, 85% of our students will show proficiency on our essential fraction standards as measured by our common summative assessment."

💡 Preventative Analysis: Anticipating Potential Reality Gaps

Take 5 minutes with your team to discuss these variables before starting instruction. Use them to guide your adjustments during team meetings.

Curriculum Alignment

Is our core curriculum strictly aligned with our essential power standards?

Instructional Pacing

Are we prioritizing our calendar space and sequencing effectively?

Assessment Formats

Are students explicitly trained on key assessment formats and specialized vocabulary?

Teaching Strategies

Have we coordinated the highest-impact practices for this specific student learning?
